Theorem 1 — Optimal Group-Specific Thresholds
Let G ∈ {A, B} be two demographic groups with base rates PG = P(T=1|G), group-specific costs cFNG, cFPG > 0, and differentiable ROC curves ROCG.

The optimal threshold τG* minimising expected Bayesian loss:
τG* = argminτ [ cFNG · FNRG(τ) · PG + cFPG · FPRG(τ) · (1−PG) ]
satisfies the first-order condition:
dTPRG / dFPRG |τG* = cFPG · (1−PG) / ( cFNG · PG )
The optimal threshold is the point on group G's ROC curve where its slope equals the cost-adjusted base rate ratio.
Proof
Differentiating 𝓛G(τ) with respect to τ and setting to zero:
−cFNG · PG · dTPRG/dτ + cFPG · (1−PG) · dFPRG/dτ = 0
Dividing by dFPRG/dτ yields the first-order condition. □
Corollary
When PA ≠ PB or cFNA/cFPA ≠ cFNB/cFPB, the right-hand side differs between groups, so τA* ≠ τB*. A uniform threshold is therefore suboptimal for at least one group. Group-specific thresholds are not a fairness accommodation — they are the mathematically optimal decision rule.

Chouldechova's Impossibility (2017)
When PA ≠ PB, assuming equalised odds (FNRA=FNRB, FPRA=FPRB) and setting PPVA = PPVB leads to the contradiction PA = PB. Therefore calibration and equalised odds cannot be simultaneously satisfied. This optimiser prioritises equalised odds — the criterion most relevant to patient safety.
PPVG = (1−FNRG)·PG / [ (1−FNRG)·PG + FPRG·(1−PG) ]
What this tool does
This tool implements the mathematical framework from From Proxy Features to Fair Decisions: Investigating Algorithmic Bias and Threshold Correction in NHS Mental Health AI Triage (, 2026).

Given base rates and error costs for two demographic groups, it computes the optimal group-specific decision thresholds that minimise expected Bayesian loss while achieving equalised odds — and shows why calibration must be sacrificed (Chouldechova 2017).
Step-by-step
1. Set base rates PA and PB — the proportion of each group that genuinely needs care.
2. Set error costs cFN (missing someone in crisis) and cFP (unnecessary referral).
3. Adjust the Group A multiplier if young carers or isolated patients face higher risk from being missed.
4. Upload your own CSV of predicted probabilities, or use the example datasets.
5. Read off the optimal thresholds τA* and τB* from the Results tab.
6. See the ROC curves and the optimal operating points marked geometrically.
